## 3. Objective Rules and Instruments

### Livelihood
* **Rules**: Focus on primary source to funnel other income. Should be stable and long-term. 90-95% focus should be here. Do not unnecessarily increase lifestyle you cannot afford.
* **Instruments**: Job/Service, Business, Farming.

### Health
* **Rules**: Create corpus (1L to 1Cr) for emergencies. Funds must be available quickly. Replenish if depleted. Take Health Insurance and grow cover every 3-5 years. Do not let policy lapse. Add wife and child as family grows.
* **Instruments**: Health Insurance, Fixed Deposit, Liquid/Debt Mutual Fund (T+2).

### Life
* **Rules**: Cover should be 10x to 20x of annual income. Grow cover as risk grows. Assess risk every 2-3 years. Take Term Plan.
* **Instruments**: Term Plan, ULIP, Endowment Plan.

### Retirement
* **Rules**: Maintain lifestyle after retirement. Long-term investments (1 to 4Cr corpus). Use guaranteed return instruments and beat inflation. Use compounding. Maximize tax saving plans. Low risk.
* **Instruments**: Pension Plan/NPS, PPF, Endowment Plan, Tax Saving FDs.

### Child, Education & Marriage
* **Rules**: Plan from birth to schooling to higher education to marriage. Expenses grow with child. Start early to use compounding. Plan child's priority and use long-term horizons for withdrawals at different intervals.
* **Instruments**: ELSS, Endowment Plan, Mutual Fund (SIP).

### Parent Support, Legacy
* **Rules**: Prioritize objectives. Use compounding. Create good corpus to leave behind. Invest in immovable property only if surplus funds exist.
* **Instruments**: Mutual Fund (SIP/Lumpsum), Stocks, Long Term FDs, ELSS.

## 4. Investment Tools Comparison

### ULIP vs ELSS
* **Tax**: Both 80C. ULIP gains taxable. ELSS LTCG taxed at 10% above 1 lakh.
* **Charges**: ULIP has mortality, premium allocation. ELSS has exit load and fund management.
* **Liquidity**: ULIP 5-year lock-in. ELSS 3-year lock-in.
* **Risk/Return**: ULIP risk is high, return varies. ELSS risk is high, return depends on scheme (approx 12-14%).

### Endowment vs Term Plan
* **Endowment**: Life + Investment. High premium. Death benefit + sum assured + bonus on maturity. No risk. Medium return. No flexibility in investment.
* **Term**: Life coverage only. Less premium. No maturity benefit. No risk. No return. No flexibility.

### NPS Tier 1 vs Tier 2
* **Tier 1**: Lock-in until age 60. Min Rs 500. Deductions under 80C and 80CCD(1B). Partial withdrawals (25%) allowed after 3 years for specific reasons. Tax-free 60% withdrawal at maturity.
* **Tier 2**: No lock-in. Min Rs 1,000. No tax exemptions. Flexible withdrawals anytime. Withdrawals added to income and taxed.

## 5. General Axioms
* Never put all eggs in one basket.
* Higher risk, higher return.
* Invest low, Exit high.
* Understand risk appetite. Take a long-term view.
* Invest early (Power of Compounding).
* Don't eat more than what you can digest. Sensibility in investment, not greed.
* Always keep one bank which never changes.
* Read the policy before investment. Always know entry, exit, and benefit.
